## 2. General Procedures and Guidelines

### 2.1 Procedures

1. Request is on a *"first come, first serve"* basis.
2. School Records must be processed/acquired personally at the Office of the Registrar.
3. Fill up the Clearance for School Records.
4. Secure the signatures of the respective offices *(Office of the School President, Office of the College Dean, Alumni Office, Guidance Office, Student Affairs Office, and Library)*.
5. Submit the fully accomplished clearance/form at the **Window 5** of the Office of the Registrar for checking of records (if the requesting party has complete school credentials submitted in the office).
6. Payment of fees at the Cashier Window of the Accounting Office.
7. Submit the Clearance/Form at the Window 5 of the Office of the Registrar together with the official receipt for the requested document/s.
8. Get your claim slip from the Window 5 of the Office of the Registrar.
9. Claim your requested documents at the **Window 9** (releasing window) of the Office of the Registrar on the date indicated on your claim slip.

## 3. Request for Transfer Credentials

### 3.1 Procedures

1. Request is on a *"first come, first serve"* basis.
2. Transfer Credentials must be processed/acquired personally at the Office of the Registrar.
3. Fill up the Clearance for School Records form.
4. Check the Transcript of Records (valid for Transfer), Honorable Dismissal, and Certificate of Good Moral Character for documents that will be requested.
5. Secure the signatures of the respective offices *(Office of the School President, Office of the College Dean, Alumni Office, Guidance Office, Student Affairs Office, and Library)*.
6. Submit the fully accomplished clearance/form at the Window 5 of the Office of the Registrar for checking of records (if the requesting party has complete school credentials submitted in the office).
7. Payment of fees at the Cashier Window of the Accounting Office.
8. Submit the Clearance/Form at the Window 5 of the Office of the Registrar together with the official receipt for the requested document/s.
9. Get your claim slip from the Window 5 of the Office of the Registrar.
10. Claim your Honorable Dismissal and Certificate of Good Moral at **Window 9** (releasing window) of the Office of the Registrar on the date indicated on your claim slip.

### 3.2 Notes
Transcript of Records (valid for Transfer) will be available **fourteen (14) working days** after the Office of the Registrar receives the lower portion of the Honorable Dismissal from the new school.

## 10. Enrollment Data Survey

- Request for enrollment data/population survey must be done via face-to-face manner at the Office of the Registrar.
- The Requesting party/individual should present an official letter indicating the following:
  - 10.2.1. Name and signature of the requesting party/individual
  - 10.2.2. Purpose of the requested enrollment data/population
  - 10.2.3. Specific course/department of the requested enrollment data/population
  - 10.2.4. Specific semester and school year of the requested enrollment data/population
  - 10.2.5. Signature of the respective Program or Office Head/College Dean if available
- The Office of the Registrar is only allowed to provide the requesting party with the **total population** of the desired course/department for their survey.
- The Office of the Registrar is **not allowed** to provide names, contact numbers, addresses, and other personal information of the students to maintain confidentiality in accordance with **Republic Act 10173**, also known as *"The Data Privacy Act of 2012"*.

### 11.1 Request Letter Requirements
The requesting party/individual should submit a request letter indicating:

- 11.1.2. Course and year graduated
- 11.1.3. Purpose of the verification
- 11.1.4. Student/academic records submitted by the student/alumnus to the verifying party

### 11.2 Submission
Request letter may be submitted personally at the Office of the Registrar or sent via email through the official email address: **registrarsoffice@gmail.com**

### 11.3 Release
The letter of verification will be given personally to the requesting party if requested personally, and will be sent via electronic mail if requested online.

### 11.4 Information Verified
The Office of the Registrar will only verify the following information of the student/alumnus:
- 11.4.1. Full Name
- 11.4.2. Course Taken
- 11.4.3. Year started and ended
- 11.4.4. Date of Graduation and Special Order Number (if graduated)
- 11.4.5. Units Earned (if not graduated)

## 12. Certificate of Special Class

### 12.1 Eligibility
A student is only eligible to process a Certificate of Special Class if the student is:
- 12.1.1. Graduating in the immediate term
- 12.1.2. Having a subject deficiency(ies) certified by the Office of the Registrar
- 12.1.3. Having subject(s) offered for the current term that conflicts with other enrolled subjects

### 12.2 Stipulated Conditions by the School for Special Class
- 12.2.1. The student should fully shoulder the financial obligation of paying for the class of at least **fifteen (15) students** for the whole term, whether completed the period or not.
- 12.2.2. The student has to comply with the school and **CHED** Requirements of allowed subject loads or overloads including subject pre-requisites.
- 12.2.3. The student has to attend his/her class conscientiously like any other ordinary class.
- 12.2.4. The student is only allowed a maximum number of **ten (10) hours** of excused and unexcused absences; and **four and one half (4.5) hours** during the summer term. If incurs more than the allowed number of absences, the student may be dropped from the course.
- 12.2.5. The student may pass or fail the subject according to his/her academic performance as recorded in class.
- 12.2.6. The student may not make any special arrangement with the teacher in any form to earn a passing grade explicitly or implicitly.

## 16. Requisition of Academic Records for Dismissed/Transferred Students

### 16.1 General Policy
The Institution upholds the principles of proper records custody, data privacy, institutional accountability, and regulatory compliance. Once academic records have been formally transferred, the receiving institution assumes primary responsibility for the safekeeping, certification, and subsequent release of such records.

### 16.2
Upon official transfer and release of the Certificate of Transfer Credential (Honorable Dismissal) and Transcript of Records, the requisition, certification, and release of the student's academic records *shall be the sole responsibility of the receiving institution*.

### 16.3
Accordingly, the Institution shall **NO LONGER RELEASE, RE-ISSUE, OR CERTIFY ACADEMIC RECORDS** of the transferred student, whether to the student, a third party, or any external requesting entity.

### 16.4 Coverage
This policy applies to all former students of the Institution who meet ALL of the following conditions:
- 16.4.1. The student has been officially dismissed or cleared for transfer
- 16.4.2. A Certificate of Transfer Credential (Honorable Dismissal) and Transcript of Records have been issued by the Institution
- 16.5. The academic records have been officially transmitted to another higher education institution or authorized receiving school

### 16.6 Exceptional Circumstances
As an exception to the general rule, the Institution may consider the release of academic records ONLY under the following circumstances:
- 16.6.1. A government agency, employer, professional body, or foreign evaluation authority **EXPLICITLY REQUIRES** that the academic records be issued directly by the Institution; and
- 16.6.2. The requirement is clearly stated in a written request, official notice, or documentary proof issued by the requesting agency or company.

### 16.7 Requirement for Certificate of No Objection
In cases falling under the exceptional circumstances above, the former student shall be required to secure and submit a **CERTIFICATE OF NO OBJECTION (CNO)** from the receiving or transferred-to institution.

### 16.8 The Certificate of No Objection Must:
- 16.8.1. Be issued on the official letterhead of the receiving institution
- 16.8.2. Explicitly state that the receiving institution has NO OBJECTION to the release of the specified academic records by the Institution
- 16.8.3. Identify the purpose of the request (e.g., employment, licensure, evaluation abroad)
- 16.8.4. Be duly signed by the authorized school official
- 16.8.5. Be submitted together with the accomplished Clearance for School/Academic Records Form

### 16.9
Without a valid Certificate of No Objection, **NO ACADEMIC RECORDS SHALL BE RELEASED** under any circumstances.
